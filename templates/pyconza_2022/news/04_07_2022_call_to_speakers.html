In-person talk submissions are open

We are happy to announce that talk submissions are now open. For now we are only accepting in-person talks.

As a community-focused event, we accept, support and greatly appreciate anyone who steps up.

If you are new to public speaking it's worth knowing that PyConZA usually draws a very friendly and supportive crowd. We also have volunteers who are willing to support you as you refine your talk idea, and who can coach you in public speaking.

We have a few different kinds of talk-slots and tracks to choose from. Learn more by following the link below:

${defs.call_to_action(href='https://za.pycon.org/talks/submit-talk/',label='Everything you need to know about talk submissions')}

Important information about tickets

PyConZA 2022 is a hybrid event. That means that we support both in-person and remote attendees. We have different kinds of tickets for different kinds of attendees.

If you want to join us in Durban as an in-person attendee then you will need to buy a "Durban" ticket. And if you will be joining us remotely then you'll need an "Online" ticket.
