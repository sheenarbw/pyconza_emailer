How to join the conference

The conference officially starts at 12:00 but we suggest you try to join Discord a little bit earlier so that you can take part in the hallway track.

${defs.call_to_action(href='https://za.pycon.org/venue/discord/',label='Join our Discord server')}

The talks will be presented a little differently from last year - we'll be streaming the talks to YouTube so you will be able to watch them there; and we'll be handling audience Q&A via Discord.

Links to the talk streams will be shared on Discord and via email.

Lightning talks

Lightning talks are a space to give a short (strictly less than 5 minutes) talk to the whole conference. They're often funny, slightly strange, or informative. If you are new to talking at conferences then this is a way to stretch yourself.

If you have something you are keen to talk about then please get in touch on Discord on the #helpdesk channel; or by emailing team@za.pycon.org.

Sprints

If you are keen to hang out and build stuff then the sprints are for you.

What stuff do we build? Well, that's largely up to you. If you have something cool you are working on, or would like to work on, then please get in touch with us on #helpdesk so we can advertise your sprint topic.

The sprints are taking place in Discord on Saturday and Sunday (9 & 10 October) on the same Discord server we're using for the main conference. And you don't need a PyConZA ticket to attend, tell your friends.

Code of conduct

We have a code of conduct. It can be summarized as: be nice and respectful. People are joining this conference because the community rocks, and because they want to learn things. Think wholesome thoughts and you'll do fine :)

If you see or experience a problem, don't hesitate to let us know! You have a few options here:

  • Direct message anyone with the @organizer role on Discord
  • You can email conduct@za.pycon.org
